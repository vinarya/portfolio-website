I first dabbled with the concept of FPV systems while working on Firefly, my fire fighting hexacopter. But that involved a GPS locked drone with stabilisation provided by the flight controller, somewhat like a consumer drone from DJI. I have only recently started getting into full manual FPV drones and it has been a steep learning.

My first off-the-shelf drone was DJI Mavic Pro and I still remember how nervous I was, despite having flown model planes for years. After watching people do crazy stunts on YouTube which are impossible to pull off on a stabilised drone, I decided to pull the trigger and got myself an iFlight Nazgul. Comparing Nazgul with a Mavic is like comparing a Ferrari to a Prius. It takes considerable practice and a lot of time on the simulator. I spent hundreds of hours on Velocidrone before I was confident enough to take it out on my own.

When I am not flying, I am usually tinkering with my drones or building new ones. I have a few friends who are also into FPV and we often get together to fly. It is always a blast!
