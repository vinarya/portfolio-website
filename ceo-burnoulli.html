It was in 5th grade that I began working on my first project, a basic toy truck that could move forward and backward using a switch. The thrill of creating something with my own hands and seeing it operate sparked an interest in me for electronics and robotics. Since then, I've been looking for new things to work on every summer. During school days I would spend my free time working out the details in order to make the most of the time I had in summer.

Since I had to learn most of the skills needed for the projects myself I figured it would be great I could teach others what I had learnt and help them build their own projects. This led to the foundation of my first company, Burnoulli Tech, in junior year of high school. The idea was simple â€“ to help schools all over India to launch robotics workshops and aeromodelling clubs. We started by reaching out to local schools and conducting workshops on various topics such as soldering, programming, 3D printing. The response was overwhelming and soon we had a team of passionate students who were excited to learn more about robotics and engineering. One great example of the aeromodelling clubs was the Little Angels Aeromodelling club that taught students how to apply what they learnt in the classroom and go a step further. The club also catered to students with special needs, teaching them hands-on skills to help them become financially independent and improve their quality of life.

Today, Burnoulli Tech has grown into a successful company that sells robotics platforms and all terrain vehicles. We continue to work with schools and students, helping them discover the joy of building and creating. Our hope is that through our work, we can inspire the next generation of engineers and makers.
